## The intelligence signal

Gradients do not shout. They draw attention by giving a surface so much
<strong>atmospheric depth</strong> that the eye finds it naturally, without
breaking the design language or sacrificing the premium feel we maintain
across both light and dark mode.

## The surface handshake

A gradient surface is atmospheric. It sets the mood. The content on top of it
must remain the primary focus, which means text can never compete with the
background for attention.

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-6">
  <div className="rounded-xl border border-border bg-card p-6 flex flex-col">
    <span className="font-semibold text-foreground block" style={{ fontSize: '1.25rem' }}>Light mode</span>
    <span className="uppercase block mb-3" style={{ fontSize: '12px', fontWeight: 600, letterSpacing: '0.1em', color: 'var(--muted-foreground)', marginTop: '8px' }}>Dark on light</span>
    <p className="text-sm text-muted-foreground m-0 mb-3" style={{ lineHeight: 1.6 }}>
      All text on light gradient surfaces uses the darkest shade from the
      Midnight Neutral scale at full or reduced opacity. Headings at 100%,
      descriptions at 80%, tertiary labels at 50%. Never use brand colors

    </p>
    <div className="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border flex-1 mt-auto" style={{ backgroundImage: 'var(--gradient-primary)' }}>
