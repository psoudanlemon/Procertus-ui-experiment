## Font weight

Font weight is not decorative. It establishes a functional hierarchy between
*what something is* and *what it says*. In data-dense interfaces this distinction
is the difference between comfortable scanning and visual fatigue. Weight creates
the visual anchors that give the eye somewhere to land.

## Capitalisation

The most consequential typographic rule in this system is also the simplest.
Every piece of text belongs to one of two tiers, and the capitalisation
treatment declares which one immediately.

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-6">
  <div className="rounded-xl border border-border bg-card p-6">
    <span className="font-semibold text-foreground block" style={{ fontSize: '1.5rem' }}>Structural</span>
    <span className="uppercase block mb-3" style={{ fontSize: '12px', fontWeight: 600, letterSpacing: '0.1em', color: 'var(--muted-foreground)' }}>Uppercase, Wide Tracking</span>
    <p className="text-base text-muted-foreground leading-[1.6] m-0 mb-4">
      Buttons, form labels, table column headers, sidebar group titles and badges
      are rendered in <strong>uppercase</strong> with increased letter-spacing.
      These elements form the visual anchors of the interface. They echo the
      authority of the PROCERTUS logotype and tell the user <em>where they are</em>
      and <em>what actions are available</em>.
    </p>
    <div className="flex flex-col items-start gap-2 mt-4">
      <Button variant="link" className="p-0" asChild><a href="?path=/story/components-button--default">Buttons</a></Button>

> If text describes the *frame*, uppercase it.
> If text describes the *content inside the frame*, leave it in sentence case.

<div className="mt-6 rounded-xl border-2 border-brand-primary-200 dark:border-brand-primary-800 bg-brand-primary-50 dark:bg-brand-primary-950 p-6">
  <h4 className="font-sans text-base font-bold text-brand-primary-800 dark:text-brand-primary-200 m-0 mb-3">PROCERTUS is always written in all caps.</h4>
  <p className="text-base text-brand-primary-700 dark:text-brand-primary-300 leading-[1.6] m-0">
    The brand name is a proper noun that follows the logotype's uppercase treatment.
    Wherever it appears in UI copy, documentation, alt text or metadata,
    write it as <strong>PROCERTUS</strong>, never "Procertus" or "procertus".
  </p>
</div>

### The two-word threshold

<div className="mt-8 rounded-xl border-2 border-sys-warning-300 dark:border-sys-warning-700 bg-sys-warning-50 dark:bg-sys-warning-950 p-6">
  <h4 className="font-sans text-base font-bold text-sys-warning-800 dark:text-sys-warning-200 m-0 mb-3">Never uppercase strings longer than two words.</h4>
  <p className="text-base text-sys-warning-700 dark:text-sys-warning-300 leading-[1.6] m-0 mb-4">
